GHAIM Landing Page – Hero Section
1. Purpose of the Hero

The hero is the first handshake with the user. It must instantly communicate:

GHAIM is authoritative, trusted, and high-end.
This company handles luxury corporate events; the hero must feel deliberate, clean, and confident.
Every detail matters: spacing, typography, layering, and pacing.
2. Layout & Structure
Full-screen video background: sets the atmosphere and tone.
Logo placement: immediately visible, centered or top-left, commanding presence without overpowering.
Headline: concise, confident statement of what GHAIM does.
Subtext / supporting line: optional, subtle, reinforcing credibility.
Primary action button: visible but elegant, giving user a first clear next step.
Transparent navbar: visible but not distracting.
3. User Experience & Feeling
The page should respond naturally to scrolling, without feeling mechanical.
Content unfolds gradually; nothing hits the user all at once.
Subtle movement and depth communicates refinement.
Hover interactions are micro and authoritative, not playful.
The hero must feel calm, premium, and deliberate, not modern or gimmicky.
4. Visual Hierarchy
Logo: first thing users notice, anchors trust.
Headline: second, communicates clarity of purpose.
Supporting text: builds authority and narrative.
CTA: final, guiding the user to take action.
Background: video or image sets the tone but never competes with text.
5. Micro Details That Matter
Spacing is intentional; sections feel breathable.
Depth is created by subtle layering, not shadows or excessive effects.
Text contrast ensures readability instantly.
The first scroll should feel rewarding; new content appears as if the user discovers it naturally.
6. Tone & Messaging
Authoritative, professional, and understated.
Every element communicates luxury and reliability.
There is no distraction, no gimmicks, no flashy colors or animations.
The hero is the proof of competence.

💡 Summary:

If the hero feels premium and intentional, the entire website feels premium.
If the hero feels weak or cheap, the whole brand experience is compromised.
