GHAIM Landing Page – Footer Section
1. Purpose
Provide closure to the landing experience.
Give contact info, social proof, and navigation without feeling cluttered.
Reinforce trust, authority, and premium quality.
Serve as a functional endpoint while keeping visual elegance.
2. Structure / Layout

Viewport Consideration:

Minimum 100vh not required here; footer can be shorter (70–80vh max).
Maintain ample padding: avoid cramming, let whitespace breathe.
Background subtly different from main page (off-white #F9F9F9 or muted gradient).

Content Layout (Desktop):

Section	Position / Layout	Notes
Logo & tagline	Top-left	Keep the logo simple; tagline reinforces brand promise
Navigation links	Top-right	Horizontal or vertical, grouped logically (Services / Case Studies / Contact)
Contact info	Bottom-left	Email, phone, address; consider micro-icons for clarity
Social links	Bottom-right	Subtle hover effect, Champagne Gold (#C5A059) highlight
Optional CTA	Centered	“Book a Consultation” or “Request Proposal” button

Mobile Layout:

Stack vertically with spacing: Logo → Navigation → Contact → Social → CTA
Keep clickable areas large enough for mobile (60–70px tap targets)
Collapsible sections for nav/contact links if needed
3. Visual Style
Typography: Inter / DM Sans, medium weight, readable at small sizes.
Color palette:
Background: #F9F9F9 (Off-White)
Text: #1A1A1A (Obsidian)
Accents / CTA hover: #C5A059 (Champagne Gold)
Shadows / Depth: subtle shadow under CTA button for layering
Hover effects:
Links: opacity change or slight upward motion (Framer Motion, 0.2–0.3s)
Social icons: color accent and slight scale on hover
4. Scroll / Interaction Considerations
Footer slides up smoothly from cinematic section; no abrupt jump.
Sticky micro-elements optional: e.g., a small “Back to Top” button appears after scroll past hero.
Hover / micro-interactions via Framer Motion: buttons, social icons, CTA.
Avoid unnecessary animations that distract from previous cinematic impact.
5. Layering / Depth
Element	Z-Index	Motion / Notes
Footer background	5	Subtle gradient or shadow separation
Logo + Text	10	Centered or aligned top-left
CTA Button	15	Hover lift, shadow, premium feel
Social Icons	15	Micro-interaction on hover
6. Micro-Interaction & Accessibility
Links and buttons respond instantly but smoothly (opacity, scale, color).
Keyboard navigable links.
Ensure contrast ratio meets AAA standards (text readable on background).
Optional: subtle fade-in as user scrolls to footer.
7. Final Thoughts
Footer should never feel like an afterthought.
Minimalism + clarity = premium; don’t overfill with content.
Every hover, every shadow, every spacing decision should communicate authority and polish.

💡 Enhancement Tip:

Consider a soft animated line or subtle pattern dividing footer from content above.
For extra premium feel, CTA buttons could have micro-delay entrance triggered by scroll, making them feel intentional rather than static.
